DAY 4

Comment on 5 posts in your target space

The Value Comment formula: Agree + Add Insight + Ask a Question.

Example: "Completely agree that [their point] — I've noticed the same thing while building [your project], especially around [specific detail]. Curious how you'd handle [related question]?"

This does two things: shows genuine engagement (not just "Great post!") and puts your name in front of that poster's audience.

DAY 5

Publish Post #2 — Lessons-learned / hot take

Unpopular opinion: most student LinkedIn profiles are optimized for looking busy, not for being findable. I had certifications, skills, projects — all real — and still showed up in zero relevant recruiter searches. Why? My profile had none of the actual keywords recruiters search for. "Machine Learning" ≠ "React Developer." If your skills section doesn't match the job title you want, you're invisible for it — no matter how good your resume is. Fix: open your profile right now, search the exact job title you want, and check if your headline and skills actually contain those words. #CareerAdvice #LinkedInTips #TechStudents
DAY 6

Engage + expand network

  • Reply to every comment on both posts within a few hours of posting
  • Send 5 more connection requests (same targeting as Day 3)
  • Revisit any comments you left on Day 4 — reply if the poster responded
DAY 7

Review the numbers

Profile views: A jump from near-zero to double digits in a week means your headline/keywords are working. Flat numbers mean the headline still isn't specific enough.

Connection acceptance rate: Above 40-50% is healthy for cold requests with a personalized note. Below that, your message template needs to be more specific to each person.

Post engagement: Comments matter more than likes — they signal real reach, not just a passive scroll-past. If a post underperforms, the hook (first line) is usually the fix, not the whole post.
