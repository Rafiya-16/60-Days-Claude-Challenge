# Day 23 – Customer Research & MVP Blueprint Development

## Overview

Today I worked on transforming a startup validation report into a structured Customer & MVP Blueprint.

The goal was to move beyond the startup idea stage and create a practical roadmap covering:

* Customer understanding
* Buyer personas
* Pain points
* Customer journey
* MVP scope
* Product prioritization
* Go-to-market strategy
* Risk assessment
* Execution roadmap

---

## Startup Analyzed

### Blossom & Ease

A fashion-forward, symptom-aware D2C apparel brand designed specifically for women navigating menopause.

---

## Key Problem Identified

Women experiencing menopause often face:

* Hot flashes
* Night sweats
* Body shape changes
* Clothing discomfort
* Lack of age-inclusive fashion options

Current fashion brands primarily focus on younger demographics and fail to address menopause-related clothing needs.

## Buyer Persona

### Sunita Sharma

* 51 years old
* School Teacher
* Lucknow, India
* Married with adult children

#### Goals

* Look stylish and age-appropriate
* Stay cool and comfortable
* Find clothing designed for changing body shapes
* Maintain confidence after age 50

#### Challenges

* Poor fit from existing brands
* Synthetic fabrics increase discomfort
* Limited sizing options
* Lack of menopause-focused apparel

---

## Top Customer Pain Points

1. Hot flashes worsened by synthetic fabrics
2. Midlife body shape changes
3. Skin sensitivity
4. Night sweats
5. Lack of age-inclusive fashion
6. Poor sizing architecture
7. Lack of fabric transparency
8. Identity and confidence concerns
9. Expensive custom tailoring
10. Digital shopping friction

---

## Customer Journey

### Awareness

* Experiences discomfort
* Discovers educational content

### Consideration

* Reviews fabric details
* Checks sizing guidance
* Evaluates trust signals

### Purchase

* Orders first product
* Prefers WhatsApp support and COD

### Retention

* Positive comfort experience
* Repeat purchases
* Word-of-mouth referrals

---

## MVP Recommendation

### Build First

* Breathable bamboo-cotton kurtas
* Adaptive palazzo sets
* Lightweight layering shrugs
* WhatsApp catalogue
* Single-page landing page

### Do NOT Build Yet

* Mobile app
* Complex e-commerce platform
* Warehouse infrastructure
* Bulk inventory purchases

---

## Success Metrics

### Primary

* 5–10 paying customers

### Secondary

* 50+ waitlist signups

---

## MoSCoW Prioritization

### Must Have

* Thermo-regulating fabrics
* Adaptive sizing
* WhatsApp ordering
* Easy exchanges
* Realistic product photography

### Should Have

* COD support
* Educational content
* Age-positive branding

### Could Have

* Fabric swatches
* Daughter-focused gifting
* Consultation support

### Won't Have

* Physical stores
* Heavy ad spend
* Large inventory commitments

## Key Learnings

* Deep customer understanding is more valuable than adding features.
* A focused MVP reduces risk and accelerates validation.
* Solving a highly painful niche problem creates stronger differentiation.
* Customer interviews should guide product decisions before scaling.
