Interview Talking Points

Likely interview questions + how to answer them
Tell me about yourself.
Use your 30-second intro (Section 7). End with why this specific company or role interests you. Do not summarise your resume — they have it.
What's a project you're most proud of and why?
Nutriscope. Lead with what problem it solves, how you built it, what was technically hard, and what you'd do differently. A STAR structure (Situation → Task → Action → Result) works well here.
How do you learn new technologies?
Point to ABTalks: "I learn best by teaching — I run a LinkedIn series where I explain what I'm learning publicly. Writing a post about prompt engineering, for example, forced me to understand it deeply enough to simplify it."
What is your biggest weakness?
Be honest and specific: "I tend to spend more time learning before building than is ideal. I've noticed that I move faster when I commit to shipping something imperfect first, so I've been practicing that deliberately."
Why front-end / full-stack development?
"I like the feedback loop. Front-end work gives me immediate visual proof that something works — or doesn't. And full-stack means I understand why the data arrives the way it does, which makes me a better UI developer."
What's a technical challenge you solved?
Describe a specific bug or architecture decision from Nutriscope or Environmental Health Analyser. The goal is to show you can think through a problem, not just copy a solution from Stack Overflow.
Where do you see yourself in 3 years?
"I want to be a strong full-stack engineer at a product company — someone who can own a feature end to end. I'm also interested in the intersection of development and AI tools, given my current work on AI literacy content."
