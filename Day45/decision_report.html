Section 3

Assumption Buster

Assumptions you may be making:

  • That there's one "correct" video/channel out there, and finding it is the real task.
  • That more searching = more certainty (it usually just delays starting).
  • That basics can only be "strong" if the source is perfect, rather than through repetition and practice.

Biases at play:

  • Choice overload / analysis paralysis — more options are making the decision feel harder than the actual content difference between them.
  • Perfectionism bias — waiting for the "best" resource before starting, when a "good enough" one used consistently outperforms a perfect one left unwatched.

What you're definitely ignoring: the resource matters far less than consistency and practice — 6 months of an average tutorial with daily coding beats 6 months of searching for the perfect one.

Section 5

Premortem — It's 12 Months Later and It Failed

Option A — Free YouTube failed because:

  • You kept switching channels mid-topic, never finishing a sequence.
  • Basics stayed shaky because nothing built on the last video systematically.
  • Time got lost re-deciding "which video next" instead of practicing.

Early warning sign: you're on your 3rd different channel for the same topic within two weeks.

Prevention: pick one channel/playlist per topic and finish it before evaluating alternatives.

Option B — Structured path failed because:

  • You picked a course that didn't match your actual skill level (too basic or too advanced).
  • You followed it passively without building anything hands-on.
  • You stuck with a bad-fit resource too long out of sunk cost, instead of switching early.

Early warning sign: two weeks in, you can't explain what you learned without rewatching.

Prevention: pair every module with a small build — no passive-only weeks allowed.

Section 6

7-Day Test Plan

DAY 1-2
Shortlist 3 candidate resources (1 YouTube playlist, 1 structured course/roadmap, 1 official docs) — no watching yet, just scan syllabus/content list.
DAY 3-4
Pick one and complete its first module fully, then build one tiny thing from it (a function, a snippet, a mini page).
DAY 5-6
Talk to one senior/mentor or a peer already interning — ask what basics actually got tested in their interviews.
DAY 7
Decision day: if you finished Day 3-4's module without switching resources and could explain it out loud — lock it in for 6 months.
Section 7

The Verdict

Option B — One Structured Path

It wins because it directly protects the thing you're most scared of — weak basics — by forcing sequence and completion instead of endless comparison. With only 6 months, the cost of switching resources repeatedly is higher than the cost of a slightly imperfect starting choice.

This could flip if, after the 7-day test, you find structured courses feel too slow-paced for your current level — then a curated YouTube playlist with a written checklist becomes the better fit.

The hard truth: no resource will make your basics strong — only finishing one and practicing daily will.
